Hi Frans, Good to read that programming appealed to you and made you want to do an IT course. I wonder what appealed to the hz? Cybersecurity is a great field to start your own business in. Good that you already know that. I would advise you to opt for a Minor in your third year that is aimed at setting up your own company or a specialization focused on cybersecurity. We do pay attention to it in our program, but the training is used more generally. So it is important to find the motivation to maintain a more general education for four years.

Helping others with settings and problems with computers won't be a problem. Planning is something you will really need in higher education. It builds up and becomes more and more in terms of deadlines, activities that you have to plan and complete yourself in addition to your education. Spending your time well and thus following your schedule is important in order not to get too stressed and lag behind with the activities. Sometimes you can of course miss something, but if you procrastinate because it becomes too much, it can be quite a risk for passing your study. I therefore recommend that you find a method that works for you and ensures that you develop a habit that you can maintain. You will also be taught about this.
